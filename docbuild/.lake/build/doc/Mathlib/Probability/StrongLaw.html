    The strong law of large numbers #

    We prove the strong law of large numbers, in ProbabilityTheory.strong_law_ae: If X n is a sequence of independent identically distributed integrable random variables, then ‚àë i ‚àà range n, X i / n converges almost surely to ùîº[X 0]. We give here the strong version, due to Etemadi, that only requires pairwise independence.

    This file also contains the L·µñ version of the strong law of large numbers provided by ProbabilityTheory.strong_law_Lp which shows ‚àë i ‚àà range n, X i / n converges in L·µñ to ùîº[X 0] provided X n is independent identically distributed and is L·µñ.

    Implementation #

    The main point is to prove the result for real-valued random variables, as the general case of Banach-space valued random variables follows from this case and approximation by simple functions. The real version is given in ProbabilityTheory.strong_law_ae_real.

    We follow the proof by Etemadi [Etemadi, An elementary proof of the strong law of large numbers][etemadi_strong_law], which goes as follows.

    It suffices to prove the result for nonnegative X, as one can prove the general result by splitting a general X into its positive part and negative part. Consider X‚Çô a sequence of nonnegative integrable identically distributed pairwise independent random variables. Let Y‚Çô be the truncation of X‚Çô up to n. We claim that

    • Almost surely, X‚Çô = Y‚Çô for all but finitely many indices. Indeed, ‚àë ‚Ñô (X‚Çô ‚â† Y‚Çô) is bounded by 1 + ùîº[X] (see sum_prob_mem_Ioc_le and tsum_prob_mem_Ioi_lt_top).
    • Let c > 1. Along the sequence n = c ^ k, then (‚àë_{i=0}^{n-1} Y·µ¢ - ùîº[Y·µ¢])/n converges almost surely to 0. This follows from a variance control, as
      ‚àë_k ‚Ñô (|‚àë_{i=0}^{c^k - 1} Y·µ¢ - ùîº[Y·µ¢]| > c^k Œµ)
    ‚â§ ‚àë_k (c^k Œµ)^{-2} ‚àë_{i=0}^{c^k - 1} Var[Y·µ¢]    (by Markov inequality)
    ‚â§ ‚àë_i (C/i^2) Var[Y·µ¢]                           (as ‚àë_{c^k > i} 1/(c^k)^2 ‚â§ C/i^2)
    ‚â§ ‚àë_i (C/i^2) ùîº[Y·µ¢^2]
    ‚â§ 2C ùîº[X^2]                                     (see `sum_variance_truncation_le`)
    • As ùîº[Y·µ¢] converges to ùîº[X], it follows from the two previous items and Ces√†ro that, along the sequence n = c^k, one has (‚àë_{i=0}^{n-1} X·µ¢) / n ‚Üí ùîº[X] almost surely.
    • To generalize it to all indices, we use the fact that ‚àë_{i=0}^{n-1} X·µ¢ is nondecreasing and that, if c is close enough to 1, the gap between c^k and c^(k+1) is small.
